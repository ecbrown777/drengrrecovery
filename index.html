State-specific coverage notes +
Texas
Texas law requires that all tax sale surplus fund claims be filed by a licensed attorney. Non-attorneys are prohibited from charging fees for this service by statute. We handle your case through a licensed Texas attorney in our network — your experience and outcome are unchanged.
Georgia
Most Georgia counties will only release excess funds to the claimant directly or to a licensed attorney acting on their behalf. We coordinate claims through a licensed Georgia attorney — all filings, communications, and disbursements run through counsel, with your funds going directly to you.
New York
Depending on claim size and complexity, New York may require attorney representation for surplus fund claims. We route eligible New York cases through licensed counsel in our network to ensure proper filing and compliance.
Massachusetts
Massachusetts generally requires attorney involvement for surplus fund claims. We handle Massachusetts cases through a licensed attorney in our network on your behalf.
Indiana
Indiana statute restricts the assignment of surplus fund rights to third parties. We evaluate Indiana cases individually and, where a valid path exists, work through licensed counsel to pursue recovery.
Vermont
Vermont prohibits the assignment of surplus fund rights. Cases originating in Vermont are reviewed individually — contact us to discuss your specific situation and we will advise on available options.
Hawaii
Hawaii prohibits the assignment of surplus fund rights. Cases originating in Hawaii are reviewed individually — contact us to discuss your specific situation and we will advise on available options.
Kansas
Kansas properties are typically sold at the minimum bid amount required to satisfy tax debts, which means surplus funds are rarely generated. If you believe a surplus was created on a Kansas property, contact us — we will review the circumstances and advise accordingly.

Who We Serve

Two paths to recovering what's yours

Whether you are a property owner navigating this on your own or a legal professional working on behalf of a client, we have a process built for you.

Property owners and heirs

If you — or a family member — lost a home to foreclosure, tax sale, or eminent domain, there may be surplus proceeds owed to you. We trace those funds and file on your behalf, with no upfront fees and no legal expertise required on your end.

Estate and probate attorneys

Surplus funds frequently surface during estate administration. We handle the recovery process so you can focus on the broader estate. We work cleanly alongside counsel and structure referral relationships professionally, across every county we serve.

Real estate professionals

Agents, brokers, and title professionals often encounter clients who walked away from distressed properties without knowing funds were left behind. Referring those clients takes minutes — we handle everything from there.

Heirs to vacant or abandoned estates

If a parent or relative passed away leaving behind a property that went to sale, you may have a valid claim to the overage — even if the estate was never formally probated. We assess eligibility and guide you through the process.
