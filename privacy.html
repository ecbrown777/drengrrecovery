Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

Drengr Recovery Solutions LLC ("Drengr," "we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit drengrrecoverysolutions.com, submit a claim review request, or communicate with us by phone, email, or text message.

1. Information We Collect

  • Information you provide: name, email address, phone number, mailing or property address, details about a property sale or potential claim, and any other information you submit through our contact form or communications.
  • Consent and compliance tracking: when you submit our contact form, we automatically record the exact SMS consent language you agreed to, the date and time of consent, your IP address, and browser/device and page information, so we can maintain an auditable record of your opt-in as required for SMS compliance purposes.
  • Automatically collected information: IP address, browser type, device information, and pages visited, collected through standard web server logs and our hosting provider.

2. Cookies and Similar Tracking Technologies

We and our service providers use cookies, browser local storage, and similar technologies to operate and improve this site:

  • Essential/functional: required for our contact form and site security to work correctly.
  • Session and attribution tracking (GoHighLevel): our live chat widget is powered by GoHighLevel, which stores a session identifier and basic visit history (e.g., which page or source brought you here) in your browser's local storage. This supports the chat widget and helps us understand how visitors reach our site. It is tied to your browser/device, is not sold, and is not used for third-party advertising.
  • Security and performance (Cloudflare): our hosting and security provider, Cloudflare, uses cookies to protect the site from abuse. Cloudflare's site analytics beacon is cookieless and does not track you across other websites.
  • No advertising cookies: we do not use cookies for cross-site behavioral advertising and do not sell or share cookie or tracking data with third parties for marketing purposes.

You can block or delete cookies through your browser settings. Doing so may affect chat widget functionality. Because we do not use advertising cookies, no third-party ad opt-out is needed.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To research public records and evaluate whether surplus funds may be recoverable on your behalf.
  • To respond to your inquiries and communicate about your claim review or active claim.
  • To prepare claim documents and agreements you request.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

SMS opt-in data — third-party sharing: No mobile information will be shared with third parties/affiliates for marketing/promotional purposes. All other use case categories exclude text messaging originator opt-in data and consent; this information will not be shared with any third parties.

5. How We Share Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information only:

  • With licensed attorneys, notaries, or county/court offices as necessary to pursue a claim you have authorized.
  • With subcontractors and service providers who support our operations (e.g., form processing, email delivery, customer service), bound by confidentiality obligations. SMS opt-in data and consent are handled per the SMS section above and are not shared for marketing purposes.
  • When required by law, subpoena, or court order.

6. Data Security and Retention

We use commercially reasonable safeguards to protect your information and retain it only as long as necessary to provide services, meet legal obligations, and resolve disputes. Consent records — including the specific SMS opt-in language you agreed to, the consent timestamp, and your IP address — are retained separately as long as necessary to demonstrate regulatory compliance, even if other account information is later deleted.

7. Your California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you may have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to request deletion, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights. To exercise these rights, contact us using the information below.

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under 18, and we do not knowingly collect information from minors.
